## Part A: Multiple Choice

### Organization of the Nervous System

**1.** The central nervous system (CNS) consists of:
A) All nerves outside the brain
B) The brain and spinal cord
C) The sensory organs only
D) The autonomic nerves only

**2.** The peripheral nervous system (PNS) includes:
A) The brain and spinal cord
B) All nerves and ganglia outside the brain and spinal cord
C) Only motor nerves
D) Only the spinal cord

**3.** The division of the PNS that controls voluntary movements of skeletal muscles is the:
A) Autonomic nervous system
B) Parasympathetic division
C) Somatic nervous system
D) Sympathetic division

**4.** The "fight-or-flight" response is controlled by the:
A) Somatic nervous system
B) Parasympathetic division
C) Sympathetic division
D) Central nervous system only

### Neurons and Glia

**5.** Which part of a neuron receives incoming signals from other cells?
A) Axon
B) Cell body (soma)
C) Dendrites
D) Synaptic terminals

**6.** The long extension of a neuron that carries signals away from the cell body is the:
A) Dendrite
B) Axon
C) Soma
D) Myelin sheath

**7.** What is the function of myelin?
A) It produces neurotransmitters
B) It insulates the axon and speeds up signal transmission
C) It stores calcium ions
D) It connects neurons to muscles

**8.** The gaps in the myelin sheath where the action potential is regenerated are called:
A) Synapses
B) Dendrites
C) Nodes of Ranvier
D) Ganglia

**9.** An action potential is best described as:
A) A gradual decrease in voltage along the neuron
B) A rapid, all-or-none electrical signal that travels along the axon
C) A slow chemical signal
D) A resting state of the neuron

### Synaptic Transmission

A) Direct electrical contact

D) Physical touching of axons

**11.** Neurotransmitters are released from the:

B) Cell body

B) Because neurotransmitter vesicles are only in the presynaptic terminal and receptors are only on the postsynaptic membrane
