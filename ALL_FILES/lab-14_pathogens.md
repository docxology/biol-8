# Lab Protocol: Module 14 — Pathogens

## Lab Title
*Microbiology: Environmental Sampling and Bacterial Plating*

## Overview
*In this hands-on microbiology lab, students will practice aseptic technique, learn how to inoculate agar plates, and observe bacterial colony growth from various environmental samples.*

---

## Learning Objectives
- Practice aseptic technique to prevent contamination
- Identify and execute proper bacterial plating methods (streak plate)
- Inoculate agar plates with environmental samples
- Observe bacterial morphology and colony characteristics
- Understand the role of pathogens and microorganisms in the environment

## Estimated Duration
*2-3 hours (initial plating), followed by observation in 24-48 hours*

## Materials Needed
- Nutrient agar plates
- Sterile cotton swabs
- Inoculating loops
- Bunsen burners or incinerators
- Incubator

## Safety Considerations
- Biosafety Level 1 (BSL-1) precautions apply
- Wash hands thoroughly before and after lab
- Wipe down lab benches with 70% ethanol or bleach solution before and after work
- Do not open sealed plates after incubation

## Procedure
1. **Environmental Sampling**: Swab surfaces (doorknobs, phones, shoes) and gently streak onto section 1 of the agar plate.
2. **Aseptic Technique Practice**: Use sterile loops to practice the quadrant streak method for isolating individual colonies.
3. **Incubation**: Invert plates and incubate at 37°C for 24-48 hours.
