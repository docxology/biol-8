# Practice Test 11 — Answer Key

## Module 15: Cardiopulmonary System

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice

1. **A** — Deoxygenated blood from the systemic veins drains into the **right atrium** first (superior and inferior vena cava), before passing to the right ventricle.

2. **B** — The right ventricle ejects blood into the **pulmonary trunk**, which branches into the **pulmonary arteries** carrying blood to the lungs.

3. **B** — The first heart sound (“lub,” S1) is mainly the closing of the **AV (atrioventricular) valves** between atria and ventricles at the start of ventricular systole.

4. **B** — The second heart sound (“dup,” S2) is mainly the closing of the **semilunar valves** (pulmonary and aortic) at the end of ventricular systole.

5. **C** — **Arteries** carry blood **away** from the heart (whether oxygen-rich or oxygen-poor depends on which artery).

6. **C** — **Capillaries** have thin walls and are the primary site of exchange between blood and tissues.

7. **B** — The **systolic** value is the peak pressure during ventricular contraction; the lower number is diastolic.

8. **B** — **Veins**, especially in limbs, often have **valves** that reduce backflow when blood moves toward the heart.

9. **B** — Air is conducted from the upper airway through the larynx and trachea, then **bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli**.

10. **B** — On inspiration, the diaphragm contracts and moves **downward**, increasing thoracic volume.

11. **B** — **Tidal volume** is the air moved in a **normal, quiet** breath (in or out).

12. **A** — **Vital capacity** = tidal volume + inspiratory reserve volume + expiratory reserve volume (the largest volume that can be moved in one breath after maximal inspiration).

13. **C** — Gas exchange occurs in the **alveoli**, where the air–blood barrier is very thin.

14. **B** — **O₂** diffuses from alveolar air into blood; **CO₂** diffuses from blood into alveolar air to be **exhaled** (same ideas as Exam 03 Module 15).

15. **B** — Most O₂ is carried bound to **hemoglobin** in red blood cells; only a small fraction is dissolved in plasma.

16. **B** — Chemoreceptors in the medulla respond strongly to **rising CO₂** (and the associated drop in pH), which is why ventilation increases to blow off CO₂.

17. **C** — The **pulmonary artery** carries **deoxygenated** blood to the lungs; **pulmonary veins** return **oxygenated** blood to the left atrium (opposite of most systemic vessels’ oxygen pattern).

18. **B** — Exercise raises metabolic rate; **cardiac output** and **ventilation** increase to deliver more O₂ and remove more CO₂.

## Part B: Fill in the Blank

**19.** **SA node** — The sinoatrial node is the usual pacemaker, initiating depolarization that spreads through the atria.

**20.** **Systolic**; **diastolic** — Systolic = peak pressure during ventricular contraction; diastolic = pressure during ventricular relaxation between beats.

**21.** **Arteries**; **veins** — Systemic **arteries** carry blood away from the left ventricle; **veins** return blood toward the atria.

**22.** **Hemoglobin**; **vena cava** — O₂ binds to hemoglobin in RBCs; the **superior and inferior vena cava** deliver systemic venous blood to the right atrium.

**23.** **Bronchi**; **alveoli**; **capillaries** — After the trachea, air enters the main **bronchi**; gas exchange occurs in **alveoli** across **pulmonary capillary** walls.

## Part C: Short Answer

**24.** **Blood path (example full credit):**
   - Right atrium → tricuspid valve → **right ventricle** → pulmonary semilunar valve → **pulmonary trunk/arteries** → lung **capillaries** (around alveoli) → **pulmonary veins** → **left atrium** → mitral valve → **left ventricle** (and often mention aorta as next step is acceptable if the prompt’s “left side of the heart” is clearly satisfied).
   - **Exercise integration (any one valid):** e.g., increased **heart rate** and **stroke volume** raise cardiac output; **faster, deeper breathing** increases alveolar ventilation to match higher O₂ demand and CO₂ production; **more blood flow** to working muscles.

**25.** **Left ventricle** pumps oxygen-rich blood into the **systemic** circulation; the **aorta** is the large artery that receives blood from the left ventricle (accept wording that clearly identifies left ventricle + aorta).

**26.** **Blood pressure numbers:**
   - **Systolic:** Arterial pressure at **peak** ventricular **contraction** (ejection).
   - **Diastolic:** Arterial pressure when the ventricles are **relaxed** and refilling (residual pressure in arteries).

**27.** **CO₂ and homeostasis:**
   - Rising blood **CO₂** (and lower pH) is detected by central chemoreceptors; the **respiratory center** increases **breathing rate/depth** to **remove CO₂** and bring blood pH and gas levels back toward set point.
   - This is **homeostasis**: negative feedback keeps internal chemistry stable despite changing metabolism.
