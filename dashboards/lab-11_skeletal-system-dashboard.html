🧬 Bone Cell Types — The Living Framework
🏗️
Osteoblasts
Build new bone matrix. Secrete collagen and minerals. Eventually become trapped as osteocytes.
🔎
Osteocytes
Mature bone cells embedded in the matrix. Sense mechanical stress and coordinate remodeling signals.
⛏️
Osteoclasts
Break down (resorb) bone tissue. Release stored calcium back into the bloodstream when needed.

Comparative Anatomy
Human vs. mammal skeletons — homologous structures and evolutionary adaptation
🐾 Interactive Comparison — Human vs. Quadruped Mammal
Homologous Structures
Mammals share a common vertebrate ancestor. The same bones appear in every mammal skeleton — modified for different lifestyles. This is the concept of homology: structures with shared evolutionary origin, even if form and function differ.
Forelimb Homology Table
Human BoneLocationMammal HomologAdaptation
HumerusUpper armSame boneWeight-bearing in quadrupeds

PhalangesFingersSame bonesHooves = modified phalanges
Homologous vs. Analogous
Homologous structures share evolutionary origin (e.g., human arm and whale flipper — same bones, different function). Analogous structures share function but NOT origin (e.g., bat wing and insect wing). Only homologous structures are evidence for common ancestry.
