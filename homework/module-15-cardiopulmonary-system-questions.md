# Module 15: Cardiopulmonary System — Questions

1. Trace the path of a drop of blood starting from the **right atrium**, passing through the lungs, and returning to the systemic circulation via the aorta. List the major chambers, valves, and vessels involved in order.
2. What is the fundamental structural difference between an artery and a vein, and how does this relate to the differing blood pressures found in each vessel type?
3. Describe the mechanics of typical human ventilation. How does the contraction of the diaphragm lead to air flowing into the lungs?
4. Explain the difference between **Tidal Volume** and **Vital Capacity**. Why does tidal volume increase during exercise?
5. While measuring vital signs, you discover a patient has a resting blood pressure of 115/75 mm Hg and a resting pulse oximetry reading of 98%. What do the numbers 115 and 75 represent regarding the heart's contraction cycle?
6. When comparing heart rate before and after mild activity (like jumping jacks), you observe a significant increase. Explain the physiological necessity of this increase regarding oxygen delivery and carbon dioxide removal.
7. The brain's respiratory center automatically regulates breathing rate. Does it primarily respond to a drop in blood oxygen or a rise in blood carbon dioxide? How does this impact the blood's pH?
8. How do the cardiovascular system and the respiratory system work synergistically to maintain homeostasis? What would happen if one system failed to upregulate during exercise?
