# Module 17: Evolution — Study Questions

1.  How did Charles Darwin's observations on the Galapagos Islands influence his work?
2.  Define **Natural Selection** and explain its three core requirements: **Variation**, **Inheritance**, and **Differential Reproductive Success**.
3.  Explain the concept of **Fitness** in an evolutionary context.
4.  Compare **Natural Selection** with **Artificial Selection** (e.g., Selective Breeding).
5.  What is an **Adaptation**, and how does it arise in a population?
6.  Explain how **Fossil Records** provide evidence for evolution.
7.  What are **Homologous Structures**, and how do they differ from **Analogous Structures**?
8.  Define **Vestigial Structures** and provide two examples from the human body.
9.  How does **Comparative Embryology** support the theory of common descent?
10. What is the role of **Genetic Variation** (mutations and sexual reproduction) in evolution?
11. Describe the process of **Speciation** and identify common reproductive barriers.
12. Contrast **Microevolution** and **Macroevolution**.
13. Explain how **Molecular Biology** (DNA sequencing) provides the strongest evidence for evolutionary relationships.
14. What are the major mechanisms of evolution besides natural selection (e.g., genetic drift, gene flow)?
15. How does **Biogeography** explain the distribution of species across the globe?
16. Describe the concept of a **Common Ancestor** and its relationship to the tree of life.
17. What are the primary causes of **Mass Extinctions**, and how do they impact subsequent evolutionary trends?
18. Explain the importance of **Evolution** as the unifying theory of all biology.
