# Lab Protocol: Module 15 — Cardiopulmonary System

## Lab Title
*Cardiopulmonary: Heart, Vessels, Lungs, and Gas Exchange*

## Overview
*This lab integrates the cardiovascular and respiratory systems. Students relate heart anatomy and pulse/blood pressure measurements to breathing mechanics and gas exchange concepts prepared in lecture.*

---

## Learning Objectives
- Identify major heart chambers, valves, and the pulmonary vs. systemic circuits on models or diagrams
- Measure pulse and/or blood pressure and relate findings to cardiac cycle terminology
- Trace airflow from upper airway to alveoli and relate structure to gas exchange
- Explain how hemoglobin links lung loading of O₂ to tissue unloading alongside CO₂ transport

## Estimated Duration
*2–3 hours*

## Materials Needed
- Heart and lung models or high-quality diagrams
- Stethoscope and sphygmomanometer (if available), or pulse-only protocol as approved
- Timing device
- Handout or canvas with alveolar–capillary schematic

## Safety Considerations
- Disinfect earpieces and cuffs between users if shared
- Participants may opt out of blood pressure measurement; use pulse-only alternatives
- Follow all instructor guidance for human physiology activities

## Procedure
1. **Cardiac anatomy**: Trace one complete circuit on a model: body → right heart → lungs → left heart → body.
2. **Pulse and pressure**: Practice locating radial pulse; record resting rate. If using cuffs, measure blood pressure once under supervision and relate systolic/diastolic to the cardiac cycle.
3. **Ventilation**: Practice measuring tidal volume or respiratory rate before and after light activity (as directed).
4. **Integration diagram**: On the alveolar–capillary schematic, add arrows for O₂ and CO₂ diffusion and note where hemoglobin loading occurs.

## Data Collection
- Resting and (if applicable) post-activity pulse or respiratory rate
- Optional: paired blood pressure values with notation of body position

## Analysis Questions
- Why is the left ventricular wall thicker than the right?
- How does increasing breathing depth support increased muscle oxygen demand during exercise?
- Where does CO₂ leave the blood during external respiration?

## Conclusion
- State one cardiovascular and one respiratory concept you can now explain with your own diagram
- Note how Lab 15 content connects to Exam 3 review (Modules 11–15)
