# Module 16: Ecology — Study Questions

1.  Compare and contrast the terms **population**, **community**, **ecosystem**, and **biosphere**.
2.  What is the difference between an organism's **habitat** and its **niche**?
3.  Describe the characteristics of **exponential growth**. Under what conditions does it occur?
4.  Define **carrying capacity** (K) and explain why it is a key concept in **logistic growth**.
5.  What are **density-independent** vs. **density-dependent** factors that limit population growth?
6.  Explain the principle of **competitive exclusion**.
7.  Define and provide examples of **mutualism**, **commensalism**, and **parasitism**.
8.  How does energy flow through an ecosystem differently than how nutrients cycle?
9.  Explain why there are typically fewer organisms at higher **trophic levels**.
10. What is a **keystone species**, and how does its removal affect the entire community?
11. List the major terrestrial **biomes** and their primary climatic characteristics.
12. How does biodiversity contribute to the stability of an ecosystem?
13. Describe the impact of habitat fragmentation on native populations.
14. What are **invasive species**, and why are they particularly disruptive to new ecosystems?
15. Explain the role of **succession** (primary and secondary) in changing communities over time.
16. How do human activities (e.g., carbon emissions, pollution) impact the global biosphere?
17. Define **biological magnification** and explain its effect on top predators.
18. What is the importance of **decomposers** in an ecosystem's nutrient balance?
