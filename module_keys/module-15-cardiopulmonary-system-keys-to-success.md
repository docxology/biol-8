# Module 15: Cardiopulmonary System — Keys to Success

## Key Learning Objectives

### 1. Integration: Heart, Vessels, and Lungs
- Explain how pulmonary and systemic circulation link gas exchange in the lungs to gas use in tissues
- Describe how the heart, blood, and vessels transport oxygen and carbon dioxide with hemoglobin and plasma
- Relate changes in breathing depth or rate to cardiac output needs during activity

### 2. Cardiovascular System Overview
- Identify the components of the cardiovascular system and the idea of a closed double circuit
- Compare pulmonary vs. systemic circulation
- Describe the structure of the heart (chambers, valves, major vessels) and the cardiac cycle

### 3. Conduction, Cardiac Output, and Vessels
- Describe the electrical conduction pathway from SA node through ventricles
- Define cardiac output and factors that influence it
- Compare arteries, veins, and capillaries structurally and functionally; relate structure to blood pressure and exchange

### 4. Blood and Blood Pressure
- Describe blood composition and the roles of plasma, cells, and platelets
- Explain what systolic and diastolic pressures represent and basic regulation concepts
- Identify major risk factors for cardiovascular disease

### 5. Respiratory Anatomy and Ventilation
- Trace airflow through upper and lower respiratory divisions to alveoli
- Describe mechanics of inspiration and expiration (diaphragm, intercostals, pressure changes)
- Define key lung volumes and capacities

### 6. Gas Exchange, Transport, and Control of Breathing
- Explain alveolar gas exchange and factors that affect diffusion
- Describe oxygen and carbon dioxide transport in blood and the oxyhemoglobin relationship
- Explain how the brainstem and chemoreceptors adjust ventilation in response to CO₂ and O₂

---

## Study Tips

1. **Trace one red blood cell** from right atrium → lungs → left side → systemic capillary → return.
2. **Draw alveolus + capillary** and label partial pressure gradients for O₂ and CO₂.
3. **Pair concepts**: surfactant with compliance; hemoglobin with O₂ loading in lungs.
4. **Compare** sympathetic effects on heart rate vs. bronchodilation where relevant.
5. **Practice** explaining why “cardiopulmonary” is one functional unit for oxygen delivery.
